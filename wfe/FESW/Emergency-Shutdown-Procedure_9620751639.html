Firmware : Emergency Shutdown Procedure

Created by Owen Brake, last modified by Ryan Erler on Dec 12, 2021

Preliminary

Summary

Activating emergency shutdown events at various times in the car lifecycle.


There are 4 states of the car that each E-stop needs to be tested at:

  • Off - activate the E-stop when the car is off and try to turn it on.  There should be an error the moment the car is turned on
  • LV - Turn on LV and activate E-stop.  The car should get an error when the E-stop is pressed.
  • HV - Turn on the HV and activate E-stop. The car should get an error when E-stop is pressed.
  • EM enable - Turn on HV and EM enable and activate E-stop.  The car should exit EM state and get an error when E-stop is pressed.
    • Testing should also happen when the motors are running.  This should immediately stop the motors.

When an error happens, in all except 2 cases the car will get a critical error and enter error state.

The various E-stops:

  • Brake over travel switch
  • Crash switch
    • tripped by shaking it
    • reset by click
  • Driver E-stop button
    • This does not trigger a critical error.
  • Tractive system master switch
    • This does not trigger a critical error.
  • Both BRB
  • Tractive system path Positive connector interlock
  • Tractive system path Negative connector interlock
  • Charger connector interlock
  • TSMP connector interlock
  • HVD interlock
