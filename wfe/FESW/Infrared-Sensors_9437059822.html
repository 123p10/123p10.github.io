Basic Info

  • Passive infrared sensors detect motion by detecting the heat of an object
    • Made of a pyroelectric sensor which can detect levels of infrared radiation
    • Do not emit infrared radiation 
    • Small, low power and inexpensive
    • Most have a straight 3-pin header on them for connecting a cable
  • Thermal infrared sensors are generally used for thermal imaging and screening
  • Non-contact infrared sensors measures infrared light bouncing off objects without touching them to measure temperature
    • Come in an easy to use metal can and must be soldered or plugged into a breadboard
