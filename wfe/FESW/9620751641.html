Firmware : Motor / Vehicle Stress Test

Created by Owen Brake, last modified by Ryan Erler on Dec 12, 2021

Preliminary

Note: Perhaps this shouldn't be performed too often to prevent fatigue damage. But it should probably be performed at least once as the car will undergo some strong edge cases in competition.

Summary

Perform various tests to ensure that the vehicle can handle physical and electrical stress tests without damage. This is done

Tests:

  • Activating the throttle to it's maximum extent without wheels on ground.
  • Accelerating to a fast speed on the ground and braking.
  • Placing heavy load on the motor/battery, turning it off and then turning it back on after a short time and evaluating it's behavior.
    • This can be done by driving in a loop in a parking lot
    • Go to watcar skidpan at fire research center - 250m straightaway
  • On the ground, push both the brake and throttle and makes sure the car can't damage itself before the cuttoff comes in.
  • Bring the car up to speed and check the car locks all 4 wheels when the brakes are pressed.
    • First the front locks, then the rear.
  • Maximum cornering
    • By figure 8 skidpan testing
  • Slalom
    • Makes sure the car slides before it rolls.
    • Start at low speeds and go faster and faster until it either starts to roll or slide.
  • Verify cooling function works as intended
    • make sure that when the pump turns on it doesn't tank the 12V.
  • Assess the load on CAN bus
    • around 25% load is good
    • EM enable should have the most messages, make sure to log it
  • Regenerative braking
    • make sure it works
