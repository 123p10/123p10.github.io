GPS Outline

How GPS Works

  • GPS receivers capture signals from satellites orbiting the earth, which contain the time at which the signal was sent, based on atomic clocks located on the satellites.
  • This time is compared to the time clocked by the gps module itself, accounting for error, and assuming the speed of the signal sent is the speed of light, can use this to determine the distance of the module from the satellite. 
  • Using the distance from 3 satellites enables the gps to determine 2 possible points for its location: one in space and the other on Earth
    • Although 3 satellites are sufficient for triangulation, using 4 allows there to be 4 different combinations to reduce error.

 

GPS Receivers

  • The module will send messages in NMEA sentences

Programming Goals

  • Use the information transited by the GPS module to determine the speed of the car
    • We can find the speed by finding the distance between the calculated positions and using the time interval to find the speed
